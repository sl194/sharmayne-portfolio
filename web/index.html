A little about me :)

STRENGTHS
Resilience is a huge part of me. It is what enables me to continue trying and giving my best after repeated times of failure. As a judoka, the Japanese proverb 七転び八起き(i.e. fall down seven times, stand up eight) has been ingrained into me, hence I have taken failure in my stride, learning from my mistakes and always remembering to stay humble. In addition, being responsible is something that I'm known for as well, as I cannot go to sleep without following through on my commitments. When there is something that has to be done, I will get to it, and will do my best at it.
SKILLS
As an architecture student, I am trained to think critically and creatively, pay attention to detail and have good time management skills. In addition, due to my inherent fascination with all new things, I am always eager to learn and hence pick up new skills pretty quickly (in 20+++ hours, instead of 10000 hours). Although this has made me a jill-of-many-trades, it is not necessarily a bad thing, as in this day and age, it is increasingly important to be versatile and diversified! However, I do acknowledge that specialising in a field is also equally essential, hence I am currently working on that!
INTERESTS
Many things keep me up at night -- from making this website, to designing graphics, to playing the piano (with the una corda pedal locked of course), to putting together hundreds of puzzle pieces to form picteresque scenery, to catching my favourite K-reality shows/dramas -- but a commonality among these is that I enjoy engaging the right hemisphere of my brain, if the right/left brain myth is true, that is. Last but not least, I also relish imparting knowledge to others in whatever capacity I have, as a volunteer or an actual teacher.

Where I'm at now

It took me a while to realise that I enjoy design, in fact anything related to design. Since middle school, I've tried many things here and there, and eventually came to the decision to pursue an architectural education. However, I'm still learning and exploring what exactly to specialise in. Architecture? Or UI/UX? Or Marketing? I'm still undecided, hence I am actively dipping my toes in all these fields to gain more clarity.

  • Architecture: It's my undergraduate degree -- aiming to graduate with a first class honours! :)
  • UI/UX: Have dabbled in it since 2019, designing mobile applications, websites and dashboards, and it seems pretty fun and stimulating!
  • Marketing: There's a lot of numbers involved in here, much more than I thought, and profit-maximisation is the ultimate priority... But I still enjoy the art of telling stories, even if it's to sell a product/service, as long as it's not against my values I guess...
